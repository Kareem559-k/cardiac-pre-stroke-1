Cardiac Pre-Stroke

AI-synchronized ECG system for early stroke risk prediction in CAD patients

Kareem Ismail ‚Ä¢ Zeyad Ayman

Overview

Cardiovascular diseases and stroke are among the leading causes of death worldwide, often occurring without early warning symptoms. Many critical cardiac events are preceded by subtle electrical changes in the ECG that are difficult to detect using traditional diagnostic methods.

This project presents an AI-synchronized ECG system designed to detect micro-dynamic variations in cardiac signals for early stroke and heart attack risk prediction, especially in coronary artery disease (CAD) patients. By combining advanced signal processing, feature extraction, and machine learning models, the system enables continuous, non-invasive monitoring and provides accurate early risk assessment. The proposed solution aims to improve preventive cardiology, reduce late diagnosis, and support timely clinical decision-making through wearable hardware and a smart mobile application.

Problem

Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) and stroke are the leading causes of death worldwide, causing more than 18 million deaths each year. A major challenge is that many heart attacks and strokes occur without clear warning symptoms, making early detection extremely difficult.

Traditional ECG examinations are usually short, static, and performed only inside hospitals. These tests focus on obvious waveform changes, such as ST-segment abnormalities, and fail to detect subtle electrical micro-dynamic changes that appear before serious cardiac events. As a result, early ischemic and pre-stroke conditions often go unnoticed.

Additionally, advanced continuous monitoring tools such as Holter monitors and long-term ECG systems are expensive and not widely available, especially in low- and middle-income countries. Doctors often rely on limited ECG snapshots, creating a critical gap between early disease development and clinical diagnosis, which increases the risk of sudden heart attacks and strokes.

Background

Traditional ECG

Traditional ECG

Traditional ECG systems are designed to capture short, static recordings, usually taken inside hospitals or clinics. These tests focus mainly on visible waveform abnormalities, such as ST-segment elevation or rhythm disturbances.

While effective for diagnosing advanced or acute conditions, traditional ECG analysis often fails to detect early-stage cardiac risk because subtle micro-dynamic electrical changes are considered noise or remain invisible to human interpretation.

As a result, many high-risk patients‚Äîespecially those with silent ischemia‚Äîare diagnosed only after serious damage has already occurred.

AI-Based ECG

Why AI-Based ECG?

AI-based ECG analysis overcomes the limitations of traditional methods by continuously analyzing high-resolution cardiac signals and detecting micro-dynamic variations that occur before visible ECG abnormalities.

Using machine learning and advanced signal processing, AI can identify hidden patterns related to ischemia, arrhythmias, and pre-stroke risk that are impossible to detect manually.

This approach enables early prediction, continuous monitoring outside clinical settings, and personalized risk assessment‚Äîshifting diagnosis from reactive treatment to preventive care.

Conclusion

The proposed system demonstrates that micro-dynamic ECG features combined with AI can provide a strong and reliable early-warning signal for pre-stroke and ischemic risk. This supports a shift from reactive diagnosis to preventive monitoring, especially for CAD patients.

With further clinical validation and real-world deployment improvements, the approach can reduce late diagnosis, improve triage, and support timely medical decision-making through wearable monitoring and smart applications.
